The Black Panther

Black panthers are known for their exceptional night vision, which is six times better than that of humans. This gives them a significant advantage when hunting and navigating in low-light conditions.

  • Scientific Name: Panthera
  • Average Lifespan: 12 - 15 Years
  • Average Length: 1.8 to 2.1 meters
  • Habitat: Tropical and deciduous forests

The term "black panther" typically refers to a melanistic color variant of leopards (Panthera pardus) or jaguars (Panthera onca), where their normally spotted or rosetted coat appears almost entirely black due to an excess of dark pigment melanin. Despite their dark appearance, these big cats still retain their species' characteristics, such as the body structure and behaviors of regular leopards or jaguars. Black panthers are not a distinct species but rather a genetic variation within the populations of these two big cat species.
